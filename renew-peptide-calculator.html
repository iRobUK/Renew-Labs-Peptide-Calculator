How do I reconstitute lyophilised peptides?

Before you begin with reconstitution, you will need the following:

  1. Your vial of lyophilised peptide or similar molecule.
  2. A vial of bacteriostatic (bac) water.
  3. A syringe to reconstitute the peptide.

To reconstitute a lyophilised vial, follow the steps below.

  1. Pop the caps off both your vials of bac water and the lyophilised vial.
  2. Inject your syringe into the vial of bac water, pull the plunger back until the bottom of the plunger is in line with the top mark stated on the syringe. This will be either 0.5 or 1.0 mL.
  3. Inject the contents of the syringe into the lyophilised vial of peptide (or other molecule).
  4. Repeat this process until you meet the desired fill volume.
  5. If it hasn't already done so, allow the vial to sit until the peptide/molecule is fully dissolved. You may gently swirl the vial to support this process.
  6. Store the newly prepared vial in the fridge along with the opened vial of bac water. Make sure to store the vials in a sealed container.

Note - Syringes come in 0.5 and 1 mL volumes. This is the equivalent of 50 and 100 units. We recommend that you use 3 mL of bac water to reconstitute your vial; this is the equivalent of 3-6 full syringes depending on the volume of your syringe.

Which syringe size do I need?

0.5 or 1 mL syringes with a 29 or 30 gauge needle are recommended. You will also need alcohol swabs and a sharps bin. A list of products can be found here.

How do I store my peptides?

All peptides, once reconstituted need to be refrigerated. To keep the them sterile we recommend you keeping them in a closed, airtight box such as a Tupperware container.

How long will my peptides last?

Peptides have an amount of time during which they are most effective. For most products the refrigerated life is approximately 30 days.
